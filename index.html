History(历史)

This section will talk about our nation's history, from the first dinasties until the constitution of our Communist Republic.
Skip


You can find even more information at Wikipedia's "Dynasties in China" article, or to Wikipedia's "Chinese Civil War" article.

Chinese history is one of the most difficult World History branches, because of our nation's size.

China was first governed by the Xia Dynasty even though there is no definitive proof of their existance. The first dynasty that we have actual proof of its existence its the Shang Dynasty, which ruled China until they were overthrown by the Zhou.

The Zhou Dynasty ruled China while their power slowly started to fade. The Zhou government finally broke down into seven states, which are know as the Warring States. Years of wars followed this events until one of the states defeated the other six.

This state was the one governed by the Qin Dynasty. Its ruler, Shi Huang Di, proclaimed himself Emperor of China, giving birth to the Chinese Empire.


The Qin Dynasty didn't ruled for long though. It crumbled down some years after Shi Huang Di. After the Qin fall, the Han Dynasty took the power from Qin. During the Han government, China became one of the world's top economies (~ 206bC - 220aD).

The Han Dynasty eventually falled, leading to several more dynasties, until the Republic of China was instaured at the 1st day of January of 1912, with Sun Yat-sen, leader of the Kuomintang Party as president.

In 1927 the president Chiang kai-shek (from the Kuomintang Party) launched a military campaign to defeat local warlords and unify China under his control. After this events, the Communist Party of China decided to seize control over the Kuomintang leading to nearly 22 years of Civil War.

In 1949, the Communist Party of China took control of mainland China, and gave birth to the People's Republic of China, while the Kuomintang leaders and members flew to the Island of Taiwan, to create the Republic of China.
